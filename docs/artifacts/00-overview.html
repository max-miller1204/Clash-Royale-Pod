Clash Royale Post-Game Analyzer

A computer-vision + statistical-modeling pipeline that turns a Clash Royale match replay into actionable feedback: where you traded elixir poorly, which plays the model thinks were "blunders," and how the match flowed in tempo and tower-HP over time.

The two key contracts

1. The EV target

Each Interaction is a 40-frame window pairing your card placements with the opponent's. The model's training target is: 

(friendly_left_princess_Δhp + friendly_right_princess_Δhp)
  − (enemy_left_princess_Δhp + enemy_right_princess_Δhp)

Sign convention: positive = your towers came out ahead in the window. King HP is excluded (only renders when damaged).

2. The blunder rule

For each play with an EV prediction, the model looks up the training-fold median & std for that specific card (anchored on the friendly side's first card in the interaction). A play is a blunder when: 

(per_card_median − predicted_ev) / per_card_std > 1.0

In words: you played a card, and the model thinks the outcome you're about to get is more than one standard deviation worse than that card's typical outcome. Cards with fewer than 5 training samples are skipped (signal too noisy) — they already drop out of per_card_stats upstream.

Why this matters

The original pod spec asked: can we use CV + stats to grade Clash Royale decisions the way chess engines grade moves? The answer this project ships is "yes, weakly but measurably." Wave-2.5's signal-quality push took the EV model from indistinguishable from random (ρ = −0.04) to small-but-real (ρ = +0.22, ~14σ above zero on N=4,030). That's enough signal for the blunder rule to surface the worst few plays per match — which is exactly what the pod-facing deliverable is.
